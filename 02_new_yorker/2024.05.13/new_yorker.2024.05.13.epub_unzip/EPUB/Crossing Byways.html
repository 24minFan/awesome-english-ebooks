Crossing Byways

By Yusef Komunyakaa May 06, 2024

On Sunday afternoons
I’d walk the steel overpass
above fast cars, down
to the gravelly footpath
along the stony Delaware.
Did I hear someone say
“Lord, have mercy on me”
among shadows of green?
I see the two-tone pit bull,
a hefty fullback of a dog,
barrelling straight for me.
Our eyes refuse to meet
as he circles me three times
& then charges back down
the walkway to a doped-up
guy holding the chain leash,
tossing it from hand to hand.
My fists are balled up tight.
I know the dog will defend
the coward. Their silent gaze
settles on a gaggle of geese
pecking insects in the grass,
a moment between seasons.
But the dog runs up, circling
me again, & back to its owner.
I could say a swarm of words
as early evening slants to five
o’clock. The dog’s sole master
kisses him when I walk past,
headed to the stone lookout
where I’ll stand, softly calling
to gone ones, as I count shad
in the slow river’s darkling glow.
